GPU Surprise
Your iGPU Is
Sitting Idle.
Use It.
Most laptops have two GPUs. Most people only use one.
Linux lets you assign processes to each GPU individually.
Default (wasteful)
VS Code, GNOME, browser —
all running on the RTX 3060.
iGPU completely unused.

20%
dGPU wasted on desktop UI
Optimized (both GPUs working)
Desktop + editors on Intel iGPU.
RTX 3060 reserved for
rendering, compute & local LLM inference.

0%
dGPU free until you need it
# Assign VS Code to Intel iGPU
$ env DRI_PRIME=0 code-insiders

# Set system to on-demand GPU switching
$ sudo prime-select on-demand
Fair note: Windows can also set per-app GPU via Settings > Display > Graphics or NVIDIA Control Panel. I haven't tried it myself — but it's a hint, not enforcement, and apps can override it. Linux's DRI_PRIME works at the driver level, making it harder to ignore.

The Real Aha Moment
Your Monitor Cable
Chooses Your GPU
I discovered my laptop's ports are wired to different GPUs.
🔌

HDMI Port

Wired to NVIDIA RTX 3060
GPU stays active at all times
More heat, more power draw

➔
⚡

USB-C Port

Wired to Intel iGPU
NVIDIA can fully power down
Less heat, GPU free for work

$ ls -lh /sys/class/drm/card*
card0 NVIDIA → HDMI-A-1, eDP-2
card1 Intel  → DP-1, DP-2, eDP-1
The problem: When I set apps to render on the iGPU but displayed through HDMI (NVIDIA-wired), frames had to copy across GPUs via Reverse PRIME. That caused stuttering.
The fix: Swapped to USB-C (Intel-wired). Now the iGPU renders and outputs directly — no frame copying. RTX 3060 dropped to 0%.

Sometimes the best optimization is just changing a cable.

Key Takeaways
What I Learned
Today
💡
Question the OS, not just the hardware.
Linux uses ~1 GB idle vs Windows' 5 GB+. That's 4 GB back for your containers.
🎮
Know where your GPU power goes.
A text editor doesn't need an RTX 3060. Linux lets you choose per-app.
🔌
Check your cable wiring.
HDMI vs USB-C can mean the difference between GPU always-on and GPU idle.
🤔
The best upgrade is understanding your system.
Before buying more hardware, ask: is the software being efficient?
Have you tried dual-booting for development?
